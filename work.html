Education × AI

Rising Above Recall

An interactive training system that teaches teachers to move beyond memorization toward genuine cognitive rigor. Built to scale professional development without losing the nuance that matters.

Domain
K-12 Education
Timeline
2023–Present
Status
In Production

The Problem

Teachers consistently write questions that test recall rather than thinking. Traditional PD workshops don't stick — participants forget the framework within weeks.

The Approach

Interactive scenarios with immediate feedback, spaced repetition, and AI-assisted question analysis. The system adapts to each teacher's patterns.

Outcomes

  • Measurable improvement in question quality
  • Sustained engagement over traditional PD
  • Scalable without losing effectiveness
Research × Data

Cognitive Rigor Index

A national research dashboard measuring teacher cognitive rigor literacy across institutions. Turns assessment data into actionable insights for administrators and researchers.

Domain
Educational Research
Timeline
2024–Present
Scale
National

The Problem

No standardized way to measure cognitive rigor literacy across institutions. Administrators making decisions without baseline data.

The Approach

Assessment instrument design, data pipeline architecture, and visualization dashboard. Built for researchers who need rigor and administrators who need clarity.

Outcomes

  • Benchmarking across institutions
  • Longitudinal tracking capability
  • Research-grade data collection
Curriculum × Systems

AI & Cybersecurity Curriculum

Comprehensive high school curriculum bridging theoretical concepts with practical application. Designed to be teachable by non-specialists while maintaining technical accuracy.

Domain
Secondary Education
Timeline
2024
Scope
Full Year Course

The Problem

Schools need to teach AI and cybersecurity but lack curriculum that's both rigorous and accessible. Most teachers don't have technical backgrounds.

The Approach

Modular curriculum design with layered complexity. Teacher guides that anticipate questions. Hands-on projects that work without specialized equipment.

Outcomes

  • Deployable by non-specialist teachers
  • Standards-aligned and assessment-ready
  • Student engagement validated in pilots
Automation × Scale

Educational Workflow Automation

Custom AI skills that reduce administrative burden while maintaining pedagogical quality. Automates the tedious, preserves the meaningful.

Domain
Ed-Tech
Timeline
Ongoing
Type
Custom Tools

The Problem

Educators spend hours on tasks that don't require human judgment — formatting, initial drafts, data entry. Time that should go to students.

The Approach

Targeted automation of specific workflows with human review built in. AI handles the first 80%, humans handle the important 20%.

Outcomes

  • Significant time savings on routine tasks
  • Quality maintained through review loops
  • Adoption without resistance
